I am a **Master of Engineering in Robotics** student at the University of Maryland, transitioning from a background in computer science to robotics. I am drawn to the space where theory meets hardware, and I thrive in environments where a clean algorithm has to survive sensor noise, timing constraints, and the general chaos of the physical world.

My interests span **algorithms, optimization, path planning, control systems, and learning-based approaches for autonomous vehicles**. I am most excited by problems at the edge of what classical methods can handle. Currently, my core focus is on designing hybrid control architectures by specifically integrating **Model Predictive Path Integral (MPPI)** control with reinforcement learning techniques like **Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO)** to dynamically adapt cost weights.

I take a top-down approach to engineering, prioritizing rapid proof-of-concept development before breaking down the underlying mechanics. Beyond autonomous racing frameworks like F1Tenth, I am deeply interested in bridging the gap between scientific computing and physical deployment — whether applying machine vision to biological sorting systems, optimizing tensor operations on custom hardware, or analyzing data-driven Formula 1 telemetry.
